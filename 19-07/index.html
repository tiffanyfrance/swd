When precipitation falls as rain the amount is read directly from the official rain gauge and recorded in hundredths of an inch. The smallest amount of measurable precipitation is 0.01 inch with lesser amounts recorded as a trace. When the precipitation falls as snow, the snow is collected in the rain gauge, melted and measured as if it fell as rain.
